Research

How markets, machinery, and policy shape agricultural productivity

My research asks how farm households respond to changing market access, mechanization opportunities, and public policy. A central theme is how service markets help smallholders overcome scale constraints in modern agricultural production.

Mechanization and Service Markets

Agricultural mechanization, service outsourcing, and productivity

This line of work studies machinery use, custom-hired services, land productivity, maize yields, service expenditure, and non-farm employment. It connects farm-level technology adoption with broader changes in labor allocation and rural transformation.

Rural Development

Farm households, off-farm work, and rural livelihoods

A second strand examines farm couples' off-farm work decisions, land transfer choices, household energy expenditure, consumption diversity, and rural women's employment. The work emphasizes household behavior under structural transformation.

Applied Economics Energy Policy China Agricultural Economic Review

Policy and Food Systems

Food-system policy, rural industries, and agricultural resilience

Recent Chinese-language work focuses on the Greater Food approach, soybean expansion, agricultural insurance, rural industrial systems, and the policy foundations for sustaining grain production capacity.

World Agriculture Journal of Nanjing Agricultural University (Social Sciences) Agricultural Technology Economics

Methods and Data

Applied microeconometrics for agricultural economics

Across projects, I use household survey data, cross-country panels, policy variation, treatment-effect models, selection correction, and productivity analysis to study causal and behavioral mechanisms in agricultural development.
